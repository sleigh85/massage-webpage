About Medical Massage

Everyone knows that massage is great way to relax and reduce stress but it can also be used to help alleviate pain and improve medical issues related to posture, nerve compression, muscle guarding, and much more. A certified medical massage therapist has been trained to spot, test for, and treat muscular issues that often times leave people suffering needlessly because they write the pain off as them just aging or accept it as the new norm after searching for and not finding relief or answers from their doctor. In fact many doctors and physical therapists now refer their patients in for medical massage, both when a specific cause can't be identified, and as part of the rehabilitation process.

Your First Session

At your first session, the massage therapist will ask about your area of discomfort, and any diagnoes related to the discomfort. They will also ask about your medical history and any medications that you are taking to ensure that the massage techniques used are safe for you. They may do some quick assesments to check your posture, muscle engagement, and range of motion. If the therapist incoorporates multiple tools or modalities into their sessions, they will go over each and make sure that you are comfortable with any that they use. They should also go over a pressure level scale with you to ensure that you are recieving the correct pressure for an effective massage.
Once you are on the table and the session has started don't hesitate to communicate when working on an area feels painful, or creates a weird sensation. After the session your therapist will create a care plan that you can request if you ever need to. The care plan will include their findings and recommendations for treatment, achievable goals they aim for by the end of your treatment period, and any exersizes or recommendations that they advise you to follow. Care plans are updated at the end of every session and are a good indicator for how well massage therapy is working for you.

The Final Outcomes

A lot of the problems that we encounter are caused or aggrevated by the things that we do everyday so once your goals have been met and your care plan completed you will still need to follow any recommendations you've been given to keep the problem from returning in the future. These recommendations usually include specific stretches or movements, and regular maintenance massages are usually suggested monthly.
